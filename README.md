Impact on Working Capital & Mitigation Strategies
Source: Form 10-K for fiscal year ended December 31, 2024
Analysis Date: June 2025
Current Working Capital Metrics (FY25): CCC: 274 days | DSO: 94.9 days | DIO: 249.7 days | DPO: 70.2 days

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Amgen faces 12 major risk categories that collectively create significant working capital pressures. These risks primarily impact:

Accounts Receivable (DSO): Reimbursement delays, payer consolidation, biosimilar competition
Inventory (DIO): Disruptions, supply chain concentration, manufacturing challenges
Accounts Payable (DPO): Supplier consolidation, geopolitical conflicts
Cash Flow: Economic conditions, regulatory uncertainty, integration execution
Total WC Exposure: ~$45B in operating assets require careful management given elevated CCC of 274 days vs. peer median of 183 days.

RISK FACTOR 1: REIMBURSEMENT & PRICING PRESSURES
Risk Summary
U.S. Medicare price-setting (ENBREL impacted Jan 2026; Otezla Jan 2027)
State PDABs now in 8 states; 17 more introduced PDAB legislation in 2024
PBM consolidation: 6 entities control ~94% of U.S. pharmacy prescriptions
IRA inflation penalties and Part D redesign effective Jan 2025
340B Program exploitation causing revenue leakage
Working Capital Impact
Accounts Receivable (↑ pressure)

DSO Expansion Risk: 30-90 day payment delays as payers implement cost controls
Bad Debt Risk: Payer insolvency (e.g., failing state Medicaid programs)
Dispute Risk: Increased chargebacks and rebate adjustments post-billing
Current DSO of 94.9 days could expand to 110-120 days if wholesale consolidation increases
Cash Flow Deterioration

Rebate accruals reduce net receivables; IRA penalties create additional drainage
ENBREL Medicare price-cut (~40% reduction effective Jan 2026) compresses margin → less cash generation
PBM direct debit arrangements delay cash inflow 45-60 days
Inventory Pressure

Safety stock increases for slower-selling products due to restricted coverage
Obsolescence risk if payer formulary exclusions force product markdowns
Mitigation Strategies
Strategy	Implementation	Timeline	Impact
1a. Accelerate Collections	Implement supply-chain financing (SCF) for wholesalers; offer early-pay discounts (2/10 net 30)	Q2 2025	Reduce DSO by 7-10 days; cost ~1-2% discount
1b. Diversify Payer Base	Reduce PBM concentration from major 6 to top 3-4; build direct-to-plan relationships	Ongoing	Lower negotiating leverage pressure by 15-20%
1c. Dynamic Pricing Model	Implement value-based contracting (outcomes guarantees) to reduce rebate volatility	Q4 2025	Improve predictability of net AR; reduce variance
1d. Rebate Accrual Accuracy	Quarterly model updating for Medicare price-setting + state PDAB impacts	Monthly	Reduce rebate disputes by 25-30%
1e. 340B Compliance	Implement tracking & auditing; negotiate direct-to-covered-entity supply agreements	2025	Prevent unauthorized 340B use; recover ~1-2% revenue leakage
1f. AR Aging Monitoring	Set up 60+ day AR alerts; escalate to finance/legal for collection	Real-time	Catch delays early; reduce write-offs
